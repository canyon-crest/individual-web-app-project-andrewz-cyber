Characters

PS. You can press the images to view them in full size.

Zagreus — the prince of the Underworld, son of Hades and Persephone. He repeatedly attempts to escape the Underworld to learn the truth about his family and origins.

Hades — ruler of the Underworld, stern and duty-bound. He governs the realm of the dead and has a complicated relationship with his son, Zagreus.

Nyx — primordial goddess of the night. A shadowy, maternal figure in Zagreus's life who offers guidance and protection from the shadows.

Thanatos — the god/embodiment of Death. Calm and duty-focused, he enforces the rules of the dead and sometimes aids Zagreus.

Hypnos — god of Sleep and Thanatos's brother; a wry, sleepy presence around the House of Hades with a dry sense of humour.

Charon — the Underworld's ferryman and proprietor of a stern shop. He deals in passage and goods for those who can pay his price.

Megaera — one of the Furies; a fierce combatant and recurring challenger in Zagreus's escape attempts.

Alecto — one of the Fury sisters, proud and relentless in battle; she appears as a formidable opponent.

Tisiphone — the third Fury sister, driven by vengeance and intense in her fights with Zagreus.

Chaos — a primordial force who offers mysterious, often risky boons to Zagreus; an ancient and enigmatic presence.

Zeus — king of the Olympian gods; a powerful patron who offers lightning-themed aid and has important ties to the wider Olympian cast.

Poseidon — god of the sea; an Olympian patron known for wave and knockback boons and a strong personality.

Athena — goddess of wisdom and strategic warfare; an Olympian ally who provides defensive and tactical boons.

Artemis — goddess of the hunt; an Olympian who grants boons that favor precision and critical strikes.

Aphrodite — goddess of love and charm; an Olympian patron associated with charms and damage-mitigating boons.

Ares — god of war; a fierce Olympian whose boons emphasize heavy damage and combat escalation.

Dionysus — god of wine; an Olympian patron known for boons that apply lingering effects like toxin/poison.

Hermes — messenger god; offers agile, speed-and-mobility focused boons that help Zagreus move quickly through fights.

Demeter — goddess of the harvest; an Olympian patron whose powers tend to slow or chill enemies and control the battlefield.

Achilles — famed hero and a stern mentor figure. He appears as a legendary presence who trains and counsels Zagreus.

Patroclus — close companion of Achilles; a supporting character connected to the heroic stories that intersect with Zagreus's journey.

Theseus — legendary Athenian hero who appears as a major adversary in certain parts of Zagreus's escape.

Asterius — the Minotaur, a powerful warrior who often challenges Zagreus alongside Theseus in later encounters.

Dusa — a shy gorgon maid who works in the House of Hades and becomes a gentle friend and ally to Zagreus.

Skelly — a cheerful skeleton who trains Zagreus and helps teach combat basics; a friendly presence in the House.

Eurydice — an Underworld resident connected to Orpheus and brings her own personal story that intersects with Zagreus's life.

Orpheus — a famous musician of the Underworld who tells stories, sings, and has ties to Eurydice and Zagreus's circle.

Cerberus — the three-headed hound of the Underworld; loyal and protective, a memorable companion in Zagreus's home.

Sisyphus — the eternally struggling figure known for his boulder; he has his own story and interactions within the Underworld.

Bouldy — a young boulder companion related to Sisyphus; a small but charming character in the Underworld community.

Persephone — queen of the Underworld and Zagreus's mother; central to the game's emotional core and Zagreus's motivation.
